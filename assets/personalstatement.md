Since the cryptoasset market is still developing, I want to explore how the markets affect investors, firms, regulators and consumers, and how common labels used to describe cryptoassets can become inconsistent or unstable, as from my research thus far this is under-explored. As cryptoassets are often treated as one broad asset class, but a coin or token can represent many different things: exchange instruments, software projects, infrastructure exposure or even collateral objects. The existing taxonomy labels are useful but they should be tested rather than treated as ground truth. This was my main focus with my MSc dissertation exploring how meaning can be inferred without a clear defined ground truth. 

This creates a problem for risk monitoring, policy evidence, investor protection and market transparency, with the FCA's new cryptoasset regimine expected to come into force on 25 October 2027, this project is timely to address the regime shift. This means the PhD could contribute not only to investors but for regulatory evidence for consumer risk and anomaly detection. Providing value not only monetarily but to understand emerging financial harms to public bodies.
